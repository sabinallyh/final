Aftonbladet Recap is an accessible and effective news experience designed to appeal to younger audiences. It has been tailored to the target groups needs of a service where they quickly can get a summary of current events - which also gives them the possibility to immerse themselves in news they find interesting.

Read about the whole process

This project was an assignment in collaboration with Schibstedt Aftonbladet and executed as part of the Introduction to UX-design course. The task was to develop or conceptualize a news service to attract generation Z.

We started off by researching what known behaviours there were for the audience, finding statistics for other news sites demographics. Thanks to our lecturer we had access to Aftonbladet’s main insights of their user’s behaviours and could look further into what could be the cause of the data.

Before moving on to gathering quantitative and qualitative data, we wanted to define the predetermined audience, generation Z, as it’s exact age definition varies from different sources. We further narrowed the audience down to individuals aged between 15 to 23 years.

When we moved on from the social and environmental analysis we staked out the main questions we wanted answers to, and what suspicions we wanted to have confirmed. Based on those themes we came up with questions for our interviews.
